## Introduction

MatrixOne is a highly MySQL-compatible HTAP database. Using a cloud-native architecture with separated storage, compute, and transaction layers, it builds an HSTAP hyper-converged data engine that enables a single database system to support OLTP, OLAP, streaming, and other workloads simultaneously. Based on MatrixOne's strong MySQL compatibility, community members often ask during technology selection:

1. What are the advantages of MatrixOne over MySQL?
2. Can MatrixOne replace MySQL seamlessly?
3. Is MatrixOne easy enough to deploy and maintain later?
4. ...

To answer these questions, the community recently released a series of articles comparing MatrixOne and MySQL in depth, giving readers a complete understanding of MatrixOne's lightweight and easy-to-use nature.

In this series, we first showed how to deploy MatrixOne on a single machine with only one command, and introduced MatrixOne's multi-tenant capabilities to help users achieve effective resource isolation easily. In addition, through a production case from an IoT user, we demonstrated the process of smoothly migrating from MySQL to MatrixOne. The series was full of practical content and received many positive comments.
